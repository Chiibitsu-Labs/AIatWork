How to use this menu

Step 1 Pick a department.
Step 2 Find a familiar pain.
Step 3 Choose one workflow worth improving.

The goal is not to automate everything. The goal is to start where AI can help without removing human judgment.

Department menu

Choose the part of the business where work is already repeating, slowing down, or depending too much on memory.

Sales & Customer

Leads come from too many places. Replies are inconsistent. Follow-ups get missed. The same questions keep coming back.

Possible AI fits

  • FB / IG / Viber / WhatsApp inquiries ~ CRM updates + custom replies
  • Lead lists ~ personalized follow-up sequences
  • Customer questions ~ answer library in your brand voice
  • Sales calls or chat threads ~ deal summaries + next steps
  • Objections ~ response guide for the team
  • Old customer conversations ~ patterns, FAQs, and buying signals
  • Campaign ideas ~ content calendar + first drafts

What the team learns

How to give AI enough company context, keep replies human, review before sending, and stop turning every reply into a one-off manual task.

Human guardrail

AI can draft, summarize, organize, and suggest. A person should still approve pricing, promises, discounts, complaints, and relationship-sensitive replies.

Data & Reporting

Data is everywhere. Sheets are messy. Reports take too long. Numbers are available but not useful.

Possible AI fits

  • Messy raw data ~ board-ready dashboard reports
  • Receipts, invoices, expenses ~ tracker + anomaly flags
  • Spreadsheet exports ~ plain-English summary
  • Budget vs actuals ~ variance explanation
  • Sales numbers ~ trends, gaps, and next questions
  • Manual reports ~ repeatable report templates
  • Meeting notes plus numbers ~ management-ready briefing

What the team learns

How to prepare clean input, avoid screenshot chaos when a sheet or Markdown file works better, ask better questions from the numbers, and check AI output before using it.

Human guardrail

AI can organize and explain financial information. Accounting, tax, compliance, and final financial decisions still need qualified human review.

Operations

The business runs on memory. Processes live in people's heads. SOPs exist but nobody uses them. Errors repeat.

Possible AI fits

  • SOP notes ~ clean step-by-step procedures
  • Daily updates ~ operations brief + next actions
  • Task handoffs ~ checklist + owner assignment
  • Error logs ~ pattern summary
  • Recurring issues ~ improvement recommendations
  • Process notes ~ workflow map
  • Team questions ~ internal operations assistant

What the team learns

How to turn messy work into structured workflows, create reusable prompts, document once and reuse often, and build good AI habits around actual work.

Human guardrail

AI can help clarify the work. Managers still need to validate what is safe, legal, realistic, and actually usable by the team.

HR & Admin

New hires ask the same questions. Policies are hard to find. Memos take time. HR documentation is sensitive. Admin work keeps stealing focus.

Possible AI fits

  • New hire questions ~ onboarding assistant
  • Policy questions ~ staff-friendly answers
  • Memo notes ~ cleaner first draft
  • HR case notes ~ clearer documentation for review
  • Training material ~ quiz + checklist
  • Admin emails ~ polished drafts
  • Role descriptions ~ clearer responsibilities

What the team learns

How to write with clarity, protect private employee information, use AI for support without outsourcing judgment, and create review habits before anything is sent.

Human guardrail

AI should support HR drafting. It should not replace due process, confidentiality, legal review, or human responsibility.

Leadership

Too many decisions escalate to the owner. Meetings create noise. Updates are scattered. The company memory lives in one person's head.

Possible AI fits

  • Meeting audio ~ decisions + action tracker
  • Owner voice notes ~ decision memo
  • Department updates ~ weekly business brief
  • Company values ~ decision guide
  • Strategy notes ~ execution checklist
  • Recurring issues ~ leadership pattern summary
  • Scattered documents ~ searchable business memory

What the team learns

How to use AI for thinking, not just output. How to separate facts, assumptions, risks, and next actions. How to use different LLMs to challenge and audit each other.

Human guardrail

AI can sharpen a decision. It cannot carry accountability.

Logistics & Frontline

Updates come from chats, photos, calls, and handwritten notes. Frontline teams may not use the same tools as office teams. Instructions get misunderstood.

Possible AI fits

  • Field updates ~ clean daily report
  • Delivery issues ~ tracker + priority flags
  • Fuel and KM logs ~ anomaly detection
  • Inventory photos or notes ~ stock update draft
  • Supplier information ~ comparison summary
  • English instructions ~ Tagalog or Taglish frontline message
  • Safety reminders ~ simple team announcements

What the team learns

How to design AI around the tools people already use, simplify instructions, respect language/device/access limits, and avoid fancy systems the actual team will not adopt.

Human guardrail

The best AI workflow is the one the team can actually use.

Systems & Knowledge

The company needs a tracker, dashboard, portal, chatbot, or app. The process is manual but repeatable. Docs are scattered.

Possible AI fits

  • Manual process ~ simple app prototype
  • Spreadsheet tracker ~ dashboard
  • FAQs and SOPs ~ internal assistant
  • Company docs ~ searchable knowledge base
  • Form submissions ~ automated routing
  • Repeated reports ~ auto-generated summaries
  • Workflow idea ~ no-code system blueprint

What the team learns

How to choose what to build first, prepare good source material, use Markdown/docs/structured files, match the right AI tool to the job, and turn useful experiments into repeatable workflows.

Human guardrail

Build after the workflow is clear. Automation before clarity just makes the mess faster.

Use cases are only the beginning.

The real work is learning how to use AI properly.

  • Privacy and security basics
  • What not to upload
  • How to give AI useful context
  • How to prepare cleaner files
  • When to use Markdown, docs, sheets, images, or PDFs
  • How to reduce wasted tokens and messy prompts
  • Which LLM is stronger for which task
  • How to make different AI tools work together
  • How to make AI audit AI
  • How to review before sending, publishing, deciding, or building
  • How to turn one-off prompts into repeatable workflows

  • The customer question you keep answering
  • The process only one person understands
  • The follow-up that gets missed
  • The spreadsheet everyone uses but nobody fully trusts
  • The meeting that creates action items nobody tracks
  • The manual process you wish was a simple app
